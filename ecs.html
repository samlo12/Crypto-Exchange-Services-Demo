What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital or virtual form of currency that utilizes cryptography for secure financial transactions, control the creation of additional units, and verify asset transfers.

Unlike traditional currencies, cryptocurrencies are decentralized. They operate on a distributed ledger - “blockchain”. This nature eliminates the need for intermediaries including banks and governments. It provides users with better control over their financial transactions.

What are the characteristics of Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency has special characteristics and it offers a variety of benefits, including Financial Inclusion, Transparency, Security and Privacy and Borderless Transactions.

Financial Inclusion

Cryptocurrencies can bring financial services to both the unbanked and underbanked populations globally. With any digital device available for internet access, people can engage in the global economy and access various financial services without traditional banking systems.

Transparency

The blockchain technology, which is behind cryptocurrencies, enables transparent transaction by allowing anyone to view transaction information and history. It build trust and responsibility in the ecosystem of cryptocurrency.

Security and Privacy

Cryptocurrencies use advanced cryptographic techniques to secure transactions, making them difficult for fraud and hacking. Users also protect their personal information and privacy by controlling the level of information disclosure in their transactions.

Borderless Transactions

Traditional financial systems present physical barriers and delays in cross-border transactions. Cryptocurrencies enable fast and instant transfers across international boundaries. Therefore, it can eliminate the need for intermediaries, producing a lower cost.

Examples of Cryptocurrencies:

Stellar (XLM), Cardano (ADA), Chainlink (LINK), Polkadot (DOT), Litecoin (LTC), Bitcoin Cash (BCH) and EOS (EOS)

Trading Services of Cryptocurrency

Buying, selling and exchanging of digital assets are involved in trading cryptocurrencies. It enables people to speculate on their profit movements from market fluctuations. Key aspects of cryptocurrency trading services include the following:

Cryptocurrency Exchanges

There are online platforms that allow buying and selling various cryptocurrencies. Exchanges perform as intermediaries that help match buyer and seller, while a fully secure environment for transactions is provided. For example, Coinbase, Binance, and Kraken are the well-known cryptocurrency exchanges.

Trading Pairs

Trading Pairs is involved in cryptocurrency trading. It is the exchange rate between different cryptocurrencies or between a cryptocurrency and a fiat currency, such as ETH/BTC or BTC/USD.

Spot Trading

Spot trading is a common form of cryptocurrency trading. It represent the concept of buying or selling cryptocurrencies at the current market price for immediate settlement. It also means that there is direct exchange of one specific cryptocurrency to another or to a fiat currency.

Margin Trading

Margin trading allow traders to borrow funding from a cryptocurrency exchange in order to larger their trading positions. Therefore, traders can trade with more than they possess. It can lead to increased potential profits, but it also amplifies potential losses. Traders should consider their risk tolerance before engaging in margin trading.

Futures and Derivatives

Cryptocurrency futures and derivatives agreements enable traders to speculate on cryptocurrency prices without actually possessing the assets. These contracts may possess pre-established termination or expiration dates. They also provide chances for gains in both rising and falling markets.

Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)

Decentralized Exchanges (DEX) is operated on decentralized networks as a type of cryptocurrency exchange. It typically makes use of smart contracts. It removes the necessary of intermediaries, enabling the direct exchange and trading for traders. As a result, there are increased privacy and control over funds, while there may be lower liquidity compared to centralized exchanges.
